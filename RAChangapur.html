Changapur Hanuman Temple, Amravati

The Changapur Naresh Hanuman temple is the very old and famous and “Jagurt” temple dedicated to Shri Lord Hanuman. The temple is located just 1 Km on the Amravati – Walgaon Road.

Shri Kshetra Changapur Naresh Hanuman Temple is located at Changapur village comes under Kund Sarjapur panchayat belongs to Vidarbha region of Amravati district in the Indian state of Maharashtra.

It is very old and Jagrut Hanuman temple situated just 1 km on the Amravati-Walgaon Road.

The temple is set in a nice environment and having good arrangements so becomes famous around Amravati.

There is an ancient step well and another small temples in the premises.

There is a heritage cave connected to Amba Devi (Amba Nagari-Amravati). As per legend, Lord Shri Krishna used this cave path to run away Rukmani (sometimes Rukmini or Rukhamini) from her wedding ceremony. This tunnel is still in existence but it is now caved in.

Amravati is famous for Hanuman Vyayam Prasarak Mandal (HVPM), the place which is famous for its facility for different kinds of sports, established in 1914.

It is said that Amravati is named for its ancient Ambadevi temple, situated in center of the city dedicated to Goddess Amba, also known as Ekvira, and is an example of religious architecture in the Vidarbha region.

How to reach

The nearest airport is Dr. Babasaheb Ambedkar International Airport Nagpur, which is 162 KMs away.

The nearest convenient railway station is Amravati, which is 10 KMs away.

The nearest major city is Amravati, which is 5KMs away.
